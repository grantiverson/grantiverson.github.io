About >

Here you will find more information about the school

James E Sampson Memorial School is fully accredited and affiliated with the world’s largest Protestant parochial school system. We offer a well-rounded Christian curriculum for children in grades K-8th. The curriculum is constantly being evaluated and upgraded. Because our emphasis is on quality, we target the individual and try to maximize the students’ potential:

  • Individualized Instruction
  • Accelerated Kindergarten Program
  • Christian Environment
  • Strong Academic Achievements
  • National Testing
  • Fully Accredited
  • Literacy Emphasis
  • Math/Science Emphasis
  • Before and After Care
  • Community Service Program
  • Computerized Instruction
  • Weekly Hot Lunches
  • Memorable Field Trips
  • Music Lessons
  • Full Scholarships Available

Enrollment at JES is open to all families who value Christian education. We have a very family friendly environment that is very pleasant and supportive of the positive and strong academic learning experiences that occur at our school on a daily basis. We do not discriminate on the basis of race, age, gender, or denominational affiliations.

We are at present pre-enrolling for the 2012/2013 school year. Full scholarships are available through Step Up for Students and are administered by Children’s First, the funding organization. Scholarships are strictly income based and are taken from the free lunch scale in public schools.

History >

How we became James E Sampson Memorial School

James E. Sampson was 8 years old when he was taken on a school field trip with his fellow students. His school did not have an adequate play area in which to play and so he and his classmates were taken to play in an area off of Orange Ave. in Ft. Pierce. The school was then located at the Macedonia Seventh Day Adventist Church on Delaware Ave., and the grounds were unprotected and without surrounding fences.

During a ball game, James ran out to retrieve a ball and was suddenly and tragically run over by a passing automobile. James’ family and friends, despite dealing with great sorrow, with great vision and selflessness organized a community effort to make sure that what happened to James would not happen again. This effort resulted in the purchase of almost nine acres that eventually became the beautiful campus of our school, the James E Sampson Memorial School.

Philosophy >

Philosophy of education

We believe Christian education goes beyond the necessary subjects of reading, writing and math. It includes a balanced approach to developing children's physical and mental capabilities while teaching them love and respect for God. Knowledge of God and His Word forms a solid foundation to fully prepare children for life's challenges. JES is operated as an inseparable, religious-educational ministry and integrated auxiliary of the Seventh-Day Adventist Church.

Uniforms >

What to wear to school

In order to promote unity, and to encourage professionalism, JES has a school-wide dress code. Students wear red, white, and navy blue polo shirts and kaki or navy blue bottoms, including shorts, pants, skirts, skorts, and jumpers. All jackets, sweaters, and coats must be navy blue. Black, white, or brown shoes with black, white, brown, or navy blue socks are appropriate.
